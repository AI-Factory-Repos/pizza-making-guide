Learn how to prepare the perfect pizza dough using flour, water, yeast, salt, and olive oil. This step-by-step video walks you through kneading, rising, and shaping your dough for a crispy, chewy crust.

Instructions

  1. Combine 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 packet of active dry yeast, 1 tsp salt, and 1 tbsp olive oil in a large bowl.
  2. Gradually add 1 cup of warm water and mix until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise for 1–2 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and shape it into your desired pizza base.

Discover the secret to a rich, flavourful tomato sauce using San Marzano tomatoes, garlic, basil, and oregano. This quick no-cook sauce brings authentic Italian taste to every slice.

Instructions

  1. Crush one 400 g can of San Marzano tomatoes by hand or with a fork.
  2. Mix in 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 tsp dried oregano, and a handful of fresh basil leaves.
  3. Season with salt and a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and stir well. No cooking required — the oven does the work!

Master oven temperatures, pizza stone techniques, and timing to achieve a perfectly golden, crispy base with bubbling, melted cheese on top. Includes tips for both conventional and fan-assisted ovens.

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to its maximum temperature (usually 250–280 °C / 480–540 °F) for at least 30 minutes.
  2. If using a pizza stone or steel, place it in the oven during preheating.
  3. Slide the assembled pizza onto the hot stone or a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake for 8–12 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.
  5. Remove from the oven, slice, and serve immediately.
