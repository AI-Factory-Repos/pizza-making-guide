1. The Dough

Ingredients

  • 500 g (4 cups) bread flour
  • 7 g (2¼ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 325 ml (1⅓ cups) warm water (38 °C / 100 °F)
  • 10 g (2 tsp) fine sea salt
  • 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
  • 5 g (1 tsp) sugar

Method

  1. Dissolve sugar and yeast in warm water. Rest 10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix flour and salt in a large bowl. Make a well in the centre.
  3. Pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir to combine.
  4. Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Shape into a ball, oil lightly, cover and prove 1–2 hours until doubled.
  6. Divide into two 400 g dough balls. Use immediately or refrigerate up to 72 hours.

2. The Sauce

Classic Tomato Sauce

  • 400 g (14 oz) canned San Marzano tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves
  1. Crush tomatoes by hand into a bowl. Remove any tough cores.
  2. Sauté garlic in olive oil 60 seconds over medium heat.
  3. Add tomatoes, season generously. Simmer 15 minutes.
  4. Finish with torn basil. No cooking needed for authentic Neapolitan sauce.

3. Toppings

Less is more. Classic Margherita needs only three things:

Topping Amount (per pizza) Notes
Fresh mozzarella 125 g Torn, well-drained
Tomato sauce 80–100 ml Spread thinly
Fresh basil 6–8 leaves Add after baking
Extra-virgin olive oil 1 drizzle Finish before serving

4. Baking

Oven Setup

Place a pizza stone or heavy baking tray on the highest rack. Heat your oven to its maximum temperature (typically 250–280 °C / 480–535 °F) for at least 45–60 minutes before baking.

Shaping & Launching

  1. Dust a pizza peel generously with semolina or flour.
  2. Stretch dough ball to 30–32 cm by hand — no rolling pin.
  3. Add sauce, then cheese. Work quickly so dough does not stick.
  4. Slide pizza onto the hot stone. Bake 7–10 minutes until crust is charred at the edges.
