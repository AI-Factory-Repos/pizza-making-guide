    🍕 The Complete Pizza Making Guide

    From dough to delicious — everything you need to know.

    Ingredients

    Great pizza starts with quality ingredients. For a classic Neapolitan pizza you will need:

    • 500 g tipo 00 flour (or bread flour)
    • 325 ml lukewarm water
    • 10 g fine sea salt
    • 7 g active dry yeast (or 3 g instant yeast)
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

    For the sauce, use San Marzano tomatoes, fresh basil, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Keep toppings minimal so each flavour shines.

    Making the Dough

    Combine yeast, sugar, and warm water; let bloom for 5 minutes. Mix flour and salt in a large bowl, create a well, pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil, then stir until a shaggy dough forms.

    Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Alternatively, use a stand mixer with a dough hook on medium speed for 6 minutes.

    Shape into a ball, place in an oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise at room temperature for 1–2 hours until doubled.

    Fermentation

    Cold fermentation dramatically improves flavour and texture. After the initial rise, punch down the dough, divide into 2–4 balls (≈ 250 g each), place in individual oiled containers, cover, and refrigerate for 24–72 hours.

    Remove dough 1–2 hours before baking so it can come to room temperature and relax, making shaping much easier.

    Shaping the Pizza

    Flour your surface well. Press the dough ball flat with your fingers, working from the centre outward and leaving a 1 cm border for the crust. Stretch the dough over your knuckles, rotating gently, until you reach your desired size (28–30 cm is ideal).

    Avoid using a rolling pin — it degases the dough and ruins the airy crust. If the dough springs back, let it rest for 5 minutes before continuing.

    Preparing the Sauce

    Crush whole San Marzano tomatoes by hand into a bowl. Add 2 crushed garlic cloves, a handful of fresh basil, a pinch of salt, and a drizzle of olive oil. Do not cook the sauce — it will cook perfectly in the oven.

    Spread a thin, even layer of sauce over the shaped dough, leaving the border clear. Less is more; a soggy pizza is a sad pizza.

    Toppings

    Layer fresh mozzarella (torn, not sliced) over the sauce. Add any additional toppings sparingly — pepperoni, roasted vegetables, olives, or anchovies all work well. Finish with a drizzle of olive oil.

    Always add fresh herbs like basil after baking to preserve their colour and aroma.

    Baking

    Preheat your oven to its maximum temperature (typically 250–290 °C / 480–550 °F) with a pizza stone or steel on the middle rack for at least 45–60 minutes. The stone must be screaming hot.

    Transfer the topped pizza to a lightly floured pizza peel. Slide it onto the stone and bake for 7–10 minutes until the crust is golden-brown and the cheese is bubbling with charred spots.

    Serving

    Slide the pizza onto a wooden board and let it rest for 2 minutes — this keeps the base crisp. Add fresh basil, a crack of black pepper, and a final drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil before slicing.

    Cut with a sharp pizza wheel or a large knife and serve immediately. Pizza waits for no one.

    Tips & Troubleshooting

    • Dough too sticky? Add flour 1 tbsp at a time — don't overdo it.
    • Dough not rising? Check your yeast isn't expired; water should be 38–40 °C.
    • Soggy centre? Use less sauce and make sure the oven is fully preheated.
    • Burnt crust? Move the rack down or reduce temperature by 10 °C.
    • No pizza stone? Use a heavy baking sheet preheated upside-down.
