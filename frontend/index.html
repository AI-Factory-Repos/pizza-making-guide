🍕 Pizza Making Guide

Everything you need to craft the perfect pizza from scratch.

The Basics

A great pizza starts with the perfect dough. Use high-protein flour for a chewy, structured crust.

  • Flour: 500g bread flour (00 flour for Neapolitan style)
  • Water: 325ml lukewarm (65% hydration)
  • Yeast: 7g active dry yeast or 5g instant
  • Salt: 10g fine sea salt
  • Olive oil: 2 tbsp extra-virgin

Mix and knead for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cold-ferment in the refrigerator for 24–72 hours for best flavour.

A simple, uncooked tomato sauce preserves freshness and won't become bitter during baking.

  • Tomatoes: 1 can (400g) San Marzano whole peeled tomatoes
  • Garlic: 1 clove, crushed
  • Salt: ½ tsp
  • Basil: 3–4 fresh leaves, torn

Crush tomatoes by hand or blend briefly. Do not cook the sauce — let the oven do the work.

Low-moisture mozzarella is the standard for most styles, while fresh buffalo mozzarella suits Neapolitan pies.

  • Low-moisture mozzarella: Shred yourself for better melt
  • Fresh mozzarella: Tear and drain on paper towels to reduce moisture
  • Provolone: Mix with mozzarella for a sharper flavour
  • Pecorino Romano: Grate sparingly over the top after baking

Techniques & Tips

High heat is the secret to a crispy bottom and airy crust. Preheat your oven to its maximum temperature for at least 45 minutes.

  • Pizza stone: Preheat on lowest rack for 1 hour at 260°C / 500°F
  • Steel baking plate: Even better heat retention than stone
  • Bake time: 7–10 minutes at max heat until crust is golden and spotty
  • Broil finish: 60–90 seconds under broiler for top browning

Less is more. Avoid overloading the pizza — too many toppings create steam and make the crust soggy.

  1. Margherita: Tomato, mozzarella, fresh basil, olive oil
  2. Pepperoni: Tomato, mozzarella, cup-and-char pepperoni
  3. Bianca: Olive oil, ricotta, garlic, mozzarella, spinach
  4. Mushroom & truffle: White sauce, mushrooms, fontina, truffle oil
