🌡️ Oven Temperature Guidelines

Temperature is the single most important factor in achieving a crispy crust and perfectly melted cheese. Home ovens and different equipment call for slightly different ranges.

🟡

  • Longer bake time: 18–25 minutes
  • Ideal for heavily loaded toppings
  • Reduces risk of burning cheese

🍳 Equipment Comparison

Your choice of baking surface dramatically affects the final crust. Compare the two most common home-oven options below.

Pizza Stone

Pizza Stone / Steel

The professional's choice for home ovens

✅ Advantages
  • Retains and radiates intense, even heat
  • Absorbs moisture for crispier crust
  • Mimics wood-fired oven floor
  • Better oven spring and char on edges
  • Durable — lasts for years
⚠️ Considerations
  • Requires 45–60 min preheat
  • Heavy and fragile — avoid thermal shock
  • Must be dry before use
  • Higher upfront cost (~$30–$80)
Pro Tip: Pizza steel conducts heat better than stone and is more durable. If choosing between them, opt for steel.
VS
Baking Sheet

Baking Sheet / Sheet Pan

The accessible option for beginners

✅ Advantages
  • Already in most kitchens — no extra cost
  • Easy to transfer pizza in and out
  • Good for pan-style or Sicilian pizzas
  • Shorter preheat: 20–30 min
  • Lightweight and easy to handle
⚠️ Considerations
  • Less heat retention than stone/steel
  • Can produce a softer, less crispy base
  • May result in uneven browning
  • Thin pans warp at high temps
Pro Tip: Preheat your baking sheet in the oven before adding the pizza to get a crispier bottom crust.

⏱️ Preheating Instructions

Proper preheating is non-negotiable. A cold or under-heated oven is the #1 cause of soggy, undercooked pizza.

1

Position Your Rack

Place your oven rack in the lower-middle to middle position. For pizza stone/steel, place it on the rack before turning on the oven. Never put a cold stone into a hot oven — thermal shock can crack it.

← Recommended Position
Oven Interior
2

Set the Temperature

Set your oven to 450–500°F (232–260°C). If your oven has a convection setting, use it — convection circulates hot air and reduces bake time by 10–15%.

475°F Optimal Setting
3

Wait for Full Preheat

Wait until your oven signals it has reached temperature — then wait an additional 15–30 minutes. The oven thermostat beeps when the air is hot, but your stone or steel needs more time to fully absorb that heat.

Beep — air temp reached

0 min ~20 min ~45–60 min
4

Use the Broiler (Optional)

For the last 1–2 minutes of your bake, switch to broil to finish the top of the pizza. Watch it carefully — cheese can go from bubbly to burnt in seconds.

⚠️ Never leave the oven unattended when broiling. Stay close and check every 30 seconds.
5

Bake and Check Doneness

Slide your pizza onto the hot surface and bake for the time appropriate to your temperature range. A properly baked pizza has a golden-brown bottom, bubbling cheese, and lightly charred edges.
