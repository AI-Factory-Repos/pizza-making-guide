Finishing Techniques

The final touches transform a good pizza into a great one. Master these three finishing techniques to elevate every bake.

🫒

Olive Oil Drizzle

A generous drizzle of high-quality extra-virgin olive oil added immediately after baking adds richness, gloss, and depth of flavour.

  • Use a good-quality extra-virgin olive oil with fruity, peppery notes.
  • Pull the pizza from the oven and let it rest on a wire rack for 60 seconds.
  • Drizzle in a thin, circular motion from the centre outward — about 1–2 tablespoons.
  • Avoid pooling oil in the centre; keep it light and even.

🌿

Fresh Herbs

Fresh herbs added after baking retain their colour, aroma, and brightness — never wilt them in the oven.

  • Basil: Tear leaves by hand and scatter over the pizza just before serving.
  • Oregano: Rub dried or fresh leaves between your fingers to release oils, then sprinkle.
  • Rocket / Arugula: Lightly dress with lemon juice and layer on top for a peppery finish.
  • Chilli flakes: Add a pinch for heat; pair with fennel sausage or pepperoni toppings.
🎬

🧂

Final Seasoning

A last-minute seasoning pass just before serving can make every ingredient shine. Taste, then adjust.

  • Flaky sea salt: A pinch on the crust adds crunch and contrast.
  • Black pepper: Freshly cracked pepper over cheese or egg-topped pizzas is essential.
  • Lemon zest: Microplane fresh zest over seafood or vegetable pizzas for brightness.
  • Parmesan or Pecorino: Finely grate over the top just before slicing for a salty, umami hit.

Common Baking Problems & Solutions

Even experienced pizza makers encounter baking issues. Use this guide to diagnose problems and fix them before the next bake.

⚠️ Common

Soggy Bottom

Symptoms

The base of the pizza is wet, chewy, or not fully cooked even though the toppings look done. The crust lacks crispness and may feel limp when lifted.

Causes

  • Too much sauce or wet toppings releasing moisture during baking.
  • Oven or stone not preheated to full temperature before baking.
  • Dough stretched too thin in the centre.
  • Baking on a cold or damp surface.

Solutions

  • ✅ Preheat your oven and pizza stone or steel for at least 45–60 minutes at maximum temperature.
  • ✅ Spread sauce in a thin, even layer — leave a visible margin at the edge.
  • ✅ Pat wet toppings (fresh mozzarella, mushrooms, tomatoes) dry with paper towels before use.
  • ✅ Par-bake the base for 3–4 minutes before adding toppings if moisture is a persistent issue.
  • ✅ Use a pizza screen or perforated pan to allow airflow under the base.

🔴 Frustrating

Burnt Edges

Symptoms

The cornicione (crust edge) chars or burns black while the centre is still cooking. The edges may taste bitter or acrid.

Causes

  • Oven temperature too high, especially in smaller ovens with intense top heat.
  • Pizza positioned too close to the top heating element or broiler.
  • Sugar or honey in the dough recipe accelerating browning.
  • Toppings placed too close to the edge.

Solutions

  • ✅ Move the rack to the lower third of the oven to balance top and bottom heat.
  • ✅ Shield exposed crust with small strips of aluminium foil after it reaches desired colour.
  • ✅ Reduce oven temperature by 10–15 °C if you see the crust browning within the first 3 minutes.
  • ✅ Extend sauce and cheese to within 1.5 cm of the edge to protect and insulate the crust.
  • ✅ Reduce or eliminate added sugars in your dough if burning is a recurring issue.

⚠️ Common

Undercooked Centre

Symptoms

The pizza looks done on the outside but the centre of the base is doughy, pale, or gummy. Cheese may be fully melted while the dough underneath is raw.

Causes

  • Too many toppings weighing down the centre and insulating the dough from heat.
  • Dough base too thick in the centre.
  • Oven not hot enough or stone not fully preheated.
  • Opening the oven door too frequently causing heat loss.

Solutions

  • ✅ Use fewer toppings — a Neapolitan pizza uses less than you think.
  • ✅ Stretch the dough more evenly so the centre is no thicker than the rest of the base.
  • ✅ Bake directly on a preheated pizza stone or steel for maximum base heat transfer.
  • ✅ If the top is done but the base isn't, slide the pizza off the tray and onto the oven floor for 1–2 minutes.
  • ✅ Check doneness by lifting an edge with a spatula — the base should be firm and golden, not pale.
